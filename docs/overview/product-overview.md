# Product Overview

## What iFlot is

iFlot is a platform that helps logistics operators connect daily transport execution with billing and financial close.

It ensures that what is executed operationally is consistently and traceably reflected in what is invoiced.

## The role of iFlot in the operation

iFlot sits between operational execution (trips, loads, shipment documents) and financial processes (pre-billing, billing, reporting).

Its role is to ensure that both sides remain connected and consistent at all times.

It does not replace all existing systems. Instead, it provides the layer that enforces the relationship between operations and billing.

## What iFlot connects

iFlot makes the connection between key operational and financial elements explicit and traceable:

- trip execution
- cargo guide lifecycle
- billing eligibility
- financial records

By enforcing these connections, iFlot eliminates the need for manual reconciliation across systems.

## Core capabilities

At its core, iFlot provides:

### 1. Operational management

- creation and management of trips
- association of cargo guides to trips
- tracking of operational state

### 2. Lifecycle control

- explicit lifecycle states for cargo guides
- validation of operational completeness before billing
- prevention of invalid or incomplete billing scenarios

### 3. Billing integration

- generation of pre-invoices based on operational state
- direct linkage between operational records and billing
- support for corrections and reprocessing within the system

### 4. Traceability and consistency

- full audit trail from execution to invoice
- consistency between operational and financial data
- elimination of external reconciliation processes

## What iFlot is not

iFlot is not:

- a telematics or tracking platform
- a pure fleet management system
- a standalone accounting system

It does not aim to replace those systems, but to connect operational execution with billing in a consistent and traceable way.

## Expected outcome

With iFlot in place, a logistics operator can:

- execute trips and manage cargo guides within a single operational flow
- know what is billable at the moment of operational close
- generate pre-invoices without manual consolidation
- correct and reprocess records without breaking traceability
- close the month with data that is already consistent

The result is not just automation, but a system where operations and billing are inherently aligned.
