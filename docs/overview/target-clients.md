# Target Clients

## Who iFlot is built for

iFlot is designed for mid-market logistics and transport companies in LATAM that operate their own fleet and manage cargo on behalf of third-party clients.

The defining characteristic of this segment is not size alone. It is the operational gap they face: enough volume to make manual reconciliation painful, but not enough resources to justify enterprise logistics platforms.

## Profile

**Company type**  
Regional freight carriers, last-mile and distribution operators, and logistics companies offering consolidated cargo, warehousing, and delivery services.

**Fleet size**  
Companies operating roughly 50 to 150 vehicles, including owned units, trailers, and third-party resources used in regular operations.

**Operations**

- national and regional freight under cargo guides
- consolidated cargo across multiple clients and routes
- warehousing and distribution services
- last-mile delivery and parcel distribution
- continuous load dispatch and high-frequency operations

**Billing model**

Billing is tied to cargo guides and freight orders, not simply to trips or vehicle usage.

Pre-invoices are generated from completed operational records and must reflect the correct guide states at the time of billing.

**Current tooling**

Legacy operational systems, partially manual processes, or a combination of both.

Month-end close typically involves spreadsheet-based reconciliation between operational records and billing data.

## What they have in common

Regardless of specific service mix, companies in this segment share the same structural problem: operational execution and billing are not connected in a single, consistent flow.

They know what they executed. They struggle to translate that reliably into what they should invoice.

They compensate with manual work, delayed validation, and dependency on specific individuals who understand how to reconcile the system.

## What they are not looking for

Companies in this segment are not looking for better vehicle tracking or telematics.

Those capabilities are already available and can be integrated separately.

What they are looking for is a system that:

- manages cargo documentation as part of the operational flow
- enforces operational close before billing
- produces billing-ready records without manual consolidation
- ensures traceability between execution and invoice

## Evidence from the field

This profile is grounded in direct evidence from the Venezuelan transport market.

At least one logistics operator with more than 20 years of operation continues to rely on the legacy system that preceded iFlot in daily production use.

That is strong evidence that the operational problem iFlot addresses is real, persistent, and still unresolved.

Further discovery is still needed to determine how broadly this pattern applies across similar operators in the segment.

## Geographic focus

Initial focus is LATAM, with Venezuela as the first validated market.

Expansion to other markets in the region is expected as the product matures, given that ground logistics operations across LATAM share similar patterns in:

- cargo handling and consolidation
- operational workflows
- billing dependencies on execution
- regulatory and business constraints
